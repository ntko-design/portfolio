List View

B2B Feature Release | July - August 2022

List View was a feature release designed to consolidate competitor information to meet the increasing needs of Klue's experienced users. It enables users to efficiently filter out irrelevant information and highlight critical competitor news, resulting in improved workflow efficiency.

Note: The content shown in the visuals of this portfolio project have been fabricated to safeguard Klue’s customer data, and any resemblance to actual companies, products, or events is purely coincidental.

Context

How it Works

As a competitive intelligence platform, one of Klue's greatest advantages is its ability to aggregate public data about competitors into an environment called Alerts. The collected information, called Intel Cards, can be categorized based on priority, commented on to identify insights, and shared with members of users’ organizations.

Project Scope

As users became more familiar with the Alerts interface, feedback showed a desire for maximum information display and advanced categorization options. In response to the former, the List View project was initiated to condense Intel Cards into a compact vertical layout that displays the most essential information, as well as the ability to expand to reveal more context.

Precedent Analysis

My design process started by evaluating the existing design of Intel Cards, later named Default View, to identify the most useful information for users. I made three groups called critical, supplementary, and relegated, and categorized each element based on importance.

Iteration

I created multiple iterations, combining every element I considered critical with various combinations of supplementary information. The goal was to achieve a design that is information-dense yet easy to read.

Feedback Sessions

Design Review & User Tests

Through a design review session with my project manager and design director, as well as usability tests with some of Klue's most avid power users, we agreed that a preview of card content was necessary. While this decision doubled the vertical size of List View cards, it allows users to scan cards more easily without needing to expand every single card.

List View States

I worked on List View cards' states after incorporating feedback. To make room for previewed content, I redesigned the action menu to show only on mouse hover. Additionally, I determined how every card element should be rendered when List View cards are expanded.

Related Alerts

Planned Feature Release

Furthermore, I took into account the interaction between List View and a planned feature release called Related Alerts to facilitate the latter’s easy integration in the future.

Bulk Edit

Feature Release Coordination

The List View project was also developed alongside the Bulk Edit project. My design director and I coordinated to ensure cohesion in terms of design and interaction between the two projects.

Documentation

The final stage of the project involved holding reviews session with both the design and engineering teams to address any outstanding issues and assess the feasibility of the design. Following this, I documented the design specifications in preparation for its implementation.

Solution

Accessing List View

Users can freely switch between Default and List View through a menu option. List View offers a significant increase in content density, which is ideal for users who prefer to have as much information as possible on their screens.

Visibility Hierarchy

The most essential information of a card is immediately visible, such as the card title, associated companies, source, and content classification. However, some elements, like tags, are truncated and are only revealed when hovered over.

Categorizing Intel

List View cards retains the action menu from the Default View, allowing users to perform the same actions such as publishing or deleting cards when the card is hovered over. Moreover, users can apply the same actions to multiple cards by entering bulk edit mode.

Expand Options

The List View feature allows users to expand any card that catches their interest to see all of its contents. On the other hand, users can choose to view just the related alerts of the card to see relevant information.

Value

Why is it needed?

List View more than triples the amount of visible content on most common screen resolutions, and provides users with the advantage of being able to consume more information at a glance. This enhances users' workflow by enabling them to efficiently filter out irrelevant information and highlight important news regarding their competitors.

Final Thoughts

Throughout this project, I learned the importance of presenting design iterations early and often. Sharing both successful and unsuccessful design concepts allows colleagues to understand what options have been explored, and can inspire their own ideas. This, in turn, can lead to productive discussions about the project's scope, limitations, and future potential to lead to a better solution.

If there was more time to spare, I believe additional sessions could have been conducted to improve the List View design. While we had the opportunity to speak to the most passionate of users, they only represent one user group, and it is important to understand that testing with more demographics helps to consider all aspects of a design.
